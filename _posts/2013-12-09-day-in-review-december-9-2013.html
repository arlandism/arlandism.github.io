--- layout: post title: Day In Review - December 9, 2013 date: '2013-12-09T17:46:00.001-08:00' author: aml tags: modified_time: '2013-12-09T17:46:55.062-08:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-1730093662423317857.post-2069452175455278546 blogger_orig_url: https://immutablearlandis.blogspot.com/2013/12/day-in-review-december-9-2013.html --- On a brief detour from the dojo dashboard, Phil and I worked on 8th Light's customized Boucher configurations. There were hardcoded average-request-per-second and burst-limits for the webserver that our stakeholder wanted to be able to configure programmatically.

After some digging around, we figured out where Boucher was creating the nginx configuration files. From there it was simply a matter of turning the hard-coded values into variables.

To me, this story really highlighted the confidence and problem-solving skills I've gained from my time at 8th Light. Getting started, I knew very little about nginx, Chef, and Boucher, but Phil and I were quickly able to analyze which parts of these technologies were relevant to the problem at hand and swiftly find a solution we were comfortable with.
