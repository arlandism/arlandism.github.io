--- layout: post title: CS Can Be Handy date: '2014-02-09T19:38:00.000-08:00' author: aml tags: modified_time: '2014-02-09T19:38:08.609-08:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-1730093662423317857.post-9038031963440221531 blogger_orig_url: https://immutablearlandis.blogspot.com/2014/02/cs-can-be-handy.html --- On Friday, Wai Lee and I completed a really cool auto-suggest feature for a client. We implemented it using a longest-common-subsequence algorithm - it tries to compare sequences by matching their border elements, stripping them off and recursing with the stripped down version.

Wai Lee mentioned that this is a good example of where Computer Science fundamentals come in handy. Being mostly self-taught, I'm often insecure about my lack of a CS background.

There were alternative solutions to the problem but since Wai Lee was already pretty familiar with this algorithm we were able to quickly get a solution working without much effort.

I find myself thinking about this problem and how I would've solved it by myself. Most likely, I would've googled around until I came to a solution that would've been more difficult to implement and took more time. While I'm confident that with enough time and counsel I could've come up with a solution, I might've missed this relatively simple solution.

That being said, I'd really like to dig into more CS fundamentals so I'm not caught off-guard next time.
