--- layout: article title: Day In Review - October 22, 2013 date: '2013-10-22T19:38:00.002-07:00' author: aml tags: - apprenticeship modified_time: '2013-10-22T19:38:59.409-07:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-1730093662423317857.post-631330794085590004 blogger_orig_url: https://immutablearlandis.blogspot.com/2013/10/day-in-review-october-22-2013.html --- I started work on a pretty big feature today and got a bit overwhelmed at the added functionality. My mentor and I discussed a possible refactor yesterday and I decided to give it a shot today. This caused a huge loss in productivity when I ended up digging myself into a hole and trying to reach the light again.

What happened was that I got about halfway through the refactor and realized that it wouldn't work because of some type expectations I had in place. One of the drawbacks to using a heavyweight IDE like Intellij is that you let it generate all this code for you to try to get back to green during a refactor and after only a couple of minutes you're pretty entrenched. Using the "undo" command a bunch of times becomes difficult because you've touched multiple files. And if you find yourself without a recent enough commit to revert back to - like I did - then you'll find yourself clawing at dirt in full panic mode.

I think my biggest flaw today was not having a new design plan completely in place before the refactor. I just started moving towards what I thought would be an optimal solution without conceptualizing all of the expected types and layers of abstraction that would be hit by the change. I was hoping that moving stuff around would guide me to an ideal solution when I should've already had the new schema in place.

Other Things I Could've Done Better

Commit more frequently - When using Vim, I really felt the effects of changing files. Intellij really disconnects me from the process and I often forget to commit. I need to get better at this.

Use branches for big changes - This would've been a huge advantage for me today.

Maintain composure - When stuff starts to break and there's no failsafe in place I start to panic. I begin to question logical assumptions and frequently doubt myself. I need to figure out a way to get my head on straight during higher stress periods.
