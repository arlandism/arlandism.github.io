--- layout: post title: Consistent Project Styling date: '2013-12-15T11:16:00.002-08:00' author: aml tags: modified_time: '2013-12-15T18:55:52.378-08:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-1730093662423317857.post-6566077658366429425 blogger_orig_url: https://immutablearlandis.blogspot.com/2013/12/consistent-project-styling.html --- During our IPM this past Friday, Micah gave Phil and I some good insight on working collaboratively. One of the important concepts that stood out to me was consistent style.

I've found myself able to pinpoint who wrote certain parts of the dojo-dashboard codebase based on that code's styling; by styling, I mean everything from indentation to idiom. While having a distinctive programming style is fine, it doesn't serve a project well. Style inconsistencies leave future developers confused about which approach to take and increases the speed of code entropy.

Reflecting on these thoughts, I've realized that Phil and I have become guilty of the same violations. During an iteration we'll start pairing, but inevitably split off, usually mid-week. Eventually, we merge our work together with very little code review; we're usually pressed for time at this point. This is a bad practice and won't help to improve the project's code as a whole. In future iterations, I'm going to keep these kinds of nuances in mind during the estimation process.
